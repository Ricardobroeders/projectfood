# Stickiness & moat

**In one line:** Retention comes from recurring weekly *utility* — what becomes annoying when
it's absent — not from the distant "good gut, good life" payoff. Source:
[[source-brand-promise-deck]].

## Explanation
"Good gut, good life" is too distant and diffuse to drive daily return. Stickiness is what
would get harder if Project Food disappeared. The deck names two voids:
1. **Sunday gets harder (partially shipped, growing).** A weekly personalized read on what's
   missing — by nutrient, category, season — plus a small grocery list that fills the gaps.
   Without it, the weekly decision reverts to "I don't know, the usual?" The app removes one
   recurring decision.
2. **Eating out gets quieter (roadmap).** Restaurants become a chance to try plants you'd never
   cook at home, plus the table conversation. (Ties to [[concept-word-of-mouth]].)

**The underlying answer:** people don't miss the *count*; they miss that once a week the app
makes a decision they no longer have to make alone. *The 30 is what gets people in; the weekly
utility is what keeps them.*

## Why it matters to Project Food
Investment rule: **invest in recurring moments, not one-time delights.** A feature that wows
once but isn't reused is worth less than one that quietly removes friction every Sunday for
years. Useful as a prioritization lens for the roadmap.
*"Without us, Sunday gets harder."*

## Contradictions / open questions
- Void #1 is "partially shipped"; void #2 is roadmap — confirm current shipped state before
  relying on either as live.
