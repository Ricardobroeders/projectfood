# Research notes — competitor scan (2026-05)

**What it is:** Web-research notes mapping the apps that compete with Project Food on the
"30 plants a week" concept. Collected 2026-05-30.

## The headline
There is a **crowded niche of "30 plants a week" tracker apps**, plus **ZOE** at the premium
personalized-nutrition end. The number itself is table stakes; most direct competitors are
**solo checklists**. Project Food's potential edge is social + gamified + utility, not the count.

## Players
- **[[entity-zoe]]** — premium, personalized nutrition + gut/blood testing; popularized "30
  plants". Core membership ~£/$9.99/mo, Plus ~$15.99/mo; full program with test ~$294 upfront +
  $29–39/mo (as of 2026-01). AI photo food logging incl. plant variety toward 30.
- **[[entity-eating30]]** — closest direct free competitor. 500+ plant DB, quick-tap + barcode +
  Siri voice logging, achievements, "eat the rainbow" stats, iCloud sync; premium adds AI search
  and prebiotic/polyphenol tracking. iOS.
- **[[entity-30plants-ai]]** — "30 PLANTS: Eat Better"; AI photo analysis auto-detects plants in
  meals, streaks, analytics, food-collage sharing.
- **Long tail of solo trackers** (not given own pages yet): 30Veg (free checklist, UK), Plant
  Points / 30aweek.com, Thirty Plants / Thirty Plant Tracker, Fiber Tracker (Plant Power),
  Earthy30, "30 Plants" (30plants.co, AI counter).

## Implications for Project Food
- **The "30 plants" positioning alone is undifferentiated** — the brand deck positions only
  against MyFitnessPal, but the real competitive set is these trackers + ZOE. Worth reflecting in
  strategy.
- **Most rivals are single-player checklists.** Project Food's social layer (friends, Top 15
  leaderboard) and gamified joy-not-guilt design are the clearest differentiators — lean into
  them. See [[concept-word-of-mouth]], [[concept-brand-pillars]].
- **Logging breadth is a competitive gap to watch:** Eating30 offers 500+ plants + barcode +
  voice; Project Food has ~150+ tap-only. The "effortless logging" pillar is contested.
- **ZOE owns "science + personalization at a premium."** Project Food should *not* try to
  out-science ZOE (consistent with Pillar 1, "count don't preach"); compete on simplicity, joy,
  social, price (free/PWA).
