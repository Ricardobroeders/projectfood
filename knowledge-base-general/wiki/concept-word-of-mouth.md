# Word-of-mouth model

**In one line:** Growth comes from designing moments worth talking about — visible, surprising,
story-worthy — not from nagging users to share. Source: [[source-brand-promise-deck]].

## Explanation
Most food apps are private and predictable, so they generate no stories ("nobody pulls out
[[entity-myfitnesspal]] at dinner"). Project Food designs three moments that earn a story on
their own:
1. **The plant they'd never heard of.** The advice engine suggests sumac, kohlrabi, fennel
   pollen → curiosity → they buy/eat/post it.
2. **The 30/30 week.** Hitting 30 is a real, screenshot-worthy accomplishment — a casual
   humblebrag that travels.
3. **The restaurant moment (roadmap).** Snap the menu, AI surfaces which dishes pack the most
   (and most novel) plants. The table notices: "what is that app?"

## Why it matters to Project Food
This is the growth strategy. The rule: **never beg for word-of-mouth** (no "rate us 5 stars"
popups, no referral bribes) — design talkable moments, then get out of the way.
*"The best marketing is a user pointing at their phone."*
