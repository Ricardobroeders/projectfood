# Origin & science of "30 plants a week"

**In one line:** The "30 different plants a week" target comes from the American Gut Project
(published 2018) and was popularized by Tim Spector / ZOE. Source:
[[source-30plants-origin-research]].

## The evidence
The American Gut Project (>10,000 citizen scientists; Tim Spector led the UK arm) found that
people eating **30+ different plants per week** had **more diverse gut microbiomes** and more
beneficial bacteria — including SCFA producers like *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii* — than people
eating ~10. Diversity of plants (varied fibers, resistant starches, polyphenols, prebiotics)
drives diversity of microbes _(as of 2018, [[source-30plants-origin-research]])_.

## Why it matters to Project Food
- It makes the core mechanic ([[concept-30-plants-a-week]]) genuinely evidence-based — supporting
  the "true" leg of the brand promise.
- It also explains the competitive crowding: because this is **public-health guidance, not a
  proprietary idea**, many apps build on it (see [[source-competitor-scan]]). The number can't be
  owned; the experience can.
- Consistent with Pillar 1 ([[concept-brand-pillars]], "count don't preach"): the science is real
  but stays in the background — the number does the teaching.
