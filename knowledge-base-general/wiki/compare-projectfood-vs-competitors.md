# Project Food vs competitors

**Question this answers:** In a crowded "30 plants a week" market, where does Project Food
actually differentiate? Sources: [[source-competitor-scan]], [[source-30plants-origin-research]].

## Summary
The "30 plants" number is **table stakes** — it's public-health guidance ([[concept-30-plants-origin]]),
not an ownable idea, and many apps use it. ZOE owns premium science+personalization; the direct
trackers are mostly **single-player checklists**. Project Food's real wedge is **social +
gamified + joy-not-guilt + free PWA + multilingual**, plus the weekly-utility moat. Its exposure
is **logging breadth** (fewer plants, tap-only) vs rivals with bigger DBs, barcode, voice, and AI
photo logging.

## Takeaways for Project Food
- **Differentiate on social + joy, not the number.** Most rivals are solo; the friends/leaderboard
  layer is the clearest moat and matches the early data signal that friends ≈ 2× engagement
  ([[concept-engagement-drivers]]).
- **Close the logging-breadth gap** or make tap-to-log demonstrably faster — Eating30's 500+ DB +
  barcode + voice is a real threat to the "effortless" pillar ([[concept-brand-pillars]]).
- **Don't chase ZOE on science/personalization** — wrong tier, wrong pillar.
- **Lean on the free multilingual PWA** (EN/NL/IT) as reach others lack; consider the NL/EU
  market where ZOE's premium price is a barrier.

## Contradictions / open questions
- The brand deck only names MyFitnessPal as competition — it predates / omits the real "30 plants"
  field. Strategy should acknowledge this set.
- Competitor feature details are from store listings/marketing; verify social features and plant
  counts before betting on them.
