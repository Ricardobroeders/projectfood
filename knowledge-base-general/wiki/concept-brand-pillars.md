# Brand pillars

**In one line:** Four principles that govern every product and brand decision, each with
explicit "we never" guardrail rules. Source: [[source-brand-promise-deck]].

> Use the "we never" rules as an acceptance checklist when reviewing any new feature or copy.

## Pillar 1 — Count, don't preach
The number teaches; we don't lecture. *"The number is the product."*
- Never add a "Learn about your microbiome" content section.
- Never explain prebiotics, polyphenols, or short-chain fatty acids in the UI (AI advice may
  mention them naturally, never as the headline).
- Never replace the number with a vaguer score (no "gut health index", percentage, or letter
  grade). The number is 0–30.

## Pillar 2 — Different beats more
Diversity follows plant *variety*, not volume. Each plant counts once per week.
- Always count each plant once per week.
- Never reward volume or weight.
- Never frame repetition as failure ("eating kale three times is groceries").
- Always make the missing as visible as the filled, so variety is the next easy choice.
- *"Each plant counts once. What you do with it is up to you."*

## Pillar 3 — Effortless in the moment, meaningful by Sunday
Friction lives in single taps across the week, never a "log your week" chore.
- Never ask the user to recall what they ate yesterday/last week.
- Never add weight, portion, calorie, or macro fields.
- Never add a memory-dependent "weekly check-in".
- Never punish a missed day (a skipped day is an empty tap, not a broken streak).
- Always default to "right now" — easiest tap is what I'm eating today.
- Proof: tap-to-log from 150+ plants (see [[source-app-mockups]]). *"Tap as you eat. Count by
  the week."*

## Pillar 4 — Joy, not guilt
Celebrate what you ate; never scold what you didn't.
- Never use shame mechanics (no "you failed", red warning states, negative streaks).
- Never use diet-culture language ("cheat", "clean", "guilty", "naughty", "indulgent").
- Never frame the count as falling short ("22/30, 8 to go" ✓; "you're 8 short — try harder" ✗).
- Never expose what a user didn't eat as judgment ("Not yet tried" ✓; "Failed to try" ✗).
- Always lead with what they did.
- No diet labels (keto, low-carb, low-FODMAP) in the UI. *"Good food, good week, good life.
  Never homework."*

## Why it matters to Project Food
These pillars protect the [[concept-30-plants-a-week]] mechanic and directly shape the moat
([[concept-stickiness-moat]]) and growth model ([[concept-word-of-mouth]]).
